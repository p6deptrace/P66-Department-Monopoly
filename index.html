How Board Position Works

Your in-game board position is based on your weekly score, adjusted for how many scoring weeks are in the selected month. Each week ends on Friday, so depending on the calendar, a month may have either 4 or 5 scoring Fridays.

This is why a department's board position may not look exactly the same as its total score. The board is designed to show monthly progress over time, while the Department Rankings show cumulative performance and weekly averages.

Where Scores Come From

Department scores come from the Labor Cup Scorecard. For an in-depth breakdown of your score, visit the Labor Cup Scorecard document to see your points in each category.

Forecasting Departments

  • Schedule: 30%
  • Forecast: 30%
  • Labor: 30%
  • Punch: 10%

Non-Forecasting Departments

  • Schedule: 30%
  • Labor: 50%
  • Punch: 20%

Base Points and Bonus Points

Both Forecasting and Non-Forecasting departments have 100 base points. Bonus points can be earned for labor effectiveness. This bonus accounts for labor performance and does not reward understaffing, so departments should not attempt to improve their score by intentionally understaffing.

Forecasting departments also have an additional bonus opportunity tied to forecast accuracy. Because Forecasting and Non-Forecasting departments do not have identical scoring structures, the two groups compete separately and each group has its own winner.

The true maximum score for Forecasting departments is 110 points. The true maximum score for Non-Forecasting departments is 105 points.

How the Spire Works

Once a department makes it all the way around the board, top performers may move onto the Spire. The Spire highlights the highest point earners throughout the hotel.

The Spire works the same way as the board: the more points a department earns, the higher up the Spire that department moves. At the end of the month, the highest point value within each division determines that division's winner.

Division Winners and Prize Eligibility

Forecasting and Non-Forecasting departments compete separately. If a Forecasting department finishes second in its own division but has a higher score than every Non-Forecasting department, that does not make the Forecasting department eligible for the Non-Forecasting prize.

To win a prize, a department must win within its own division, as displayed in the Department Rankings.

How to Read Department Rankings

In the Department Rankings, the blue total next to each department name is the department's total points so far. This is all completed weeks added together and is what drives the department's ranking.

Below the total is the department's weekly average. This is calculated by taking all completed weekly points and dividing by the number of completed weeks so far.

If you hover your mouse over a department, a weekly breakdown will appear showing each individual week's points and status.

Group Averages

At the bottom of each department group, the Total Average shows the average total points across all departments in that group. The Week Average shows how the group performed in the most recent completed week.

These averages help departments compare their performance against the rest of their own division.

Within Reach and Statuses

The Within Reach section highlights departments that are close to moving up into the next performance tier. This helps identify teams that may be one strong week away from improving their status.

Statuses are based on a weekly scale and are explained in the Scoring Legend.

Biggest Improvements

Biggest Improvements highlights departments with the strongest week-over-week improvement. This is meant to recognize bounce-back moments and teams that improved significantly after a difficult previous week.

This section helps highlight departments that may not be in first place but are still showing strong progress and effort.
